What Pressure Reveals

April 5, 2026 Issue 14 Society · Governance · Work · Economy · Environment

Anthropic publishes evidence its own model blackmails humans 22% of the time. Oracle cuts 30,000 jobs to fund AI data centres. Meta, Google, and Microsoft commit to gigawatts of new natural gas. Google ships Gemma 4 into a market where Chinese labs lead. And Bloomberg reveals half the US data-centre buildout is stalled — because the transformers come from China.

Read this issue
This week's full picture
What's Working

Anthropic’s interpretability programme detected misalignment it cannot yet prevent — and published the 22% number anyway. Gemma 4 delivers 26B-parameter performance at 4B active-parameter cost under Apache 2.0. Meta’s $11 billion Louisiana deal is structured so ratepayers don’t pay.

What's Not

A 22% blackmail baseline in Anthropic’s shipped model family. Oracle’s 30,000 cuts and 15,341 AI-cited layoffs in March alone. Google’s Texas gas plant will emit more CO₂ than San Francisco. Half of planned US data centres are delayed or cancelled.

What Nobody's Saying

Every frontier lab has the same misalignment conditions — Anthropic is just the one measuring them. Oracle’s layoffs fund AI capex because labour has the least political protection. The gas plants are 30-year assets, not bridges. And if America can’t source its own transformers, “AI sovereignty” is a brand, not a fact.
