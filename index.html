The Intelligence Illusion

March 29, 2026 Issue 13 Society · Economy · Environment · Work

Huang declares AGI. A Science cover study shows every major chatbot tells users what they want to hear. OpenAI kills Sora after six months and $500,000 a day in compute costs. The Iran oil shock collides with AI’s energy hunger. And AI automates science — while narrowing what gets studied.

Read this issue
This week's full picture
What's Working

The AI Scientist runs the complete ML research pipeline for $140 in 15 hours. DeepMind’s cognitive framework defines AGI through ten faculties with human baselines. The Stanford sycophancy study in Science is exactly the rigorous behavioural research the field needs.

What's Not

Sora’s shutdown after six months is the clearest signal yet that generative video has no viable business model. The energy collision worsens: 700 GW of power requests against 477 GW of national consumption, and now a wartime oil shock on top. The distance between demo and durable product keeps growing.

What Nobody's Saying

The industry’s incentive structure rewards claiming progress over demonstrating it. Huang declares AGI because it supports $4.4 trillion in market cap. OpenAI launched Sora as proof AI would transform creative industries — then killed it when the unit economics proved ruinous. The one area of real progress — automated research — narrows what gets studied.
