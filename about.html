About this publication

What it is and why it exists

AI generates more noise than almost any subject in recent memory. The AI Brief cuts through it with a single discipline applied to every development: what's genuinely working, what isn't, and what's being left unsaid. Three to four developments a week, rigorously sourced, with a synthesis of what they mean in combination.

The Format

Each Sunday edition covers three to four significant AI developments from the preceding week, each anchored by a distinct primary source. Every edition closes with a "What It All Means" synthesis — where the developments are read together to identify larger patterns, tensions, and second-order implications.

The format is deliberate. Individual AI developments, taken in isolation, are often misleading — either overhyped or underweighted. The synthesis is where the genuine analytical work happens.

What We Cover

  • Governance & Policy AI regulation, government adoption, institutional accountability, and the rule of law as intelligent systems become embedded in public infrastructure.
  • Work & Organizations How AI reshapes how organizations function, how leaders manage, what gets automated, and what the next generation of professional work looks like.
  • Economy & Society The macroeconomic picture: productivity, labour markets, inequality, cost of living, and who benefits from — or bears the cost of — the AI transition.
  • Environment & Planet The energy and water footprint of the AI build-out, the trade-offs with climate commitments, and AI's contested role in climate solutions.

Editorial Standards

  • Every claim is sourced. Every source is linked. Readers are expected and encouraged to verify.
  • We do not accept advertising or sponsorship. We have no commercial interest in any technology we cover.
  • We distinguish clearly between what the evidence shows and what advocates claim.
  • We treat complexity, nuance, and uncertainty as features, not obstacles.
  • When evidence is thin, contested, or absent, we say so. We do not fill gaps with confident-sounding speculation.

The Full Picture Lens

This publication applies an organizational and systemic lens to AI — asking not just "what happened" but "what does this mean for how institutions function, how decisions get made, and what the second-order effects are likely to be."

Our analytical framework is The Full Picture Lens. Every synthesis examines three dimensions:

  • What's Working — Genuine progress backed by evidence. When something works, we say so — no reflexive caveats.
  • What's Not — Failures, overpromises, and gaps between claims and reality. Named specifically, not generically.
  • What Nobody's Saying — Uncomfortable truths, perverse incentives, and structural problems that don't fit neat narratives.

The principles behind this lens:

  • Balance as discipline — not "both sides" centrism, but evidence-weighted honesty.
  • Good news earns its place — real progress deserves recognition, not dismissal.
  • Bad news earns specificity — failures are named precisely, not gestured at.
  • Ugly truths earn their name — structural problems and perverse incentives are surfaced, not smoothed over.
  • Research-grounded, not news-driven — academic sources and primary evidence are prioritized over press coverage.
